Design Notes - Overall Facility Layout:

  • Material Flow: Linear flow from Receiving ‚Üí Assembly ‚Üí Testing ‚Üí Shipping minimizes backtracking and WIP

Drawing 2: Assembly Floor Detailed Layout with Robot Paths

Design Notes - Assembly Floor Robot Coordination:

  • Robot Density: 17 active robots on floor + 3 charging = 85% utilization target (industry best practice)
  • Collision Avoidance: 4D path planning (x, y, z, time) with 5-second lookahead prevents deadlocks
  • Work Cell Design: Each station sized for 3-5 robots working in parallel without interference
  • Main Aisle: 10-foot clearance allows 2 robots to pass simultaneously, with priority given to loaded robots
  • Charging Strategy: Robots charge opportunistically during low-demand periods (nighttime, between shifts)
  • Human-Robot Collaboration: Station 5 and 7 demonstrate safe human-robot collaboration zones with speed/force limiting
  • Material Flow: Components staged near each station minimize travel time and maximize robot utilization

Drawing 3: Material Flow & Logistics Diagram

Design Notes - Material Flow & Logistics:

  • Bottleneck: Supplier lead times (8-16 weeks) are the primary constraint, not production capacity
  • WIP Strategy: Minimal WIP (2 days total) reduces capital tied up in inventory while maintaining flow
  • Safety Stock: 60-day raw materials buffer protects against supplier disruptions (key for USA-only sourcing)
  • Pull System: Production triggered by customer orders (make-to-order), not forecast-based push
  • Quick Wins:
    • Negotiate VMI (Vendor-Managed Inventory) with top 5 suppliers to reduce lead times 20%
    • Implement Kanban for high-usage fasteners and consumables
    • Add AGVs in Phase 2 to eliminate forklift delays and improve material flow speed
